New s-Coordinate¶

According to Kirby et al. (2022, in preparation), the governing equations in a generalized \(s\)-system \((x,y,s,t)\), with a range \(0 \leq s \leq 1\) that preserves the boundary locations from the \(\sigma\) coordinate mapping, can be expressed by

\[\begin{split} D_{,t} + ( D u_\alpha )_{,\alpha} + \tilde{\omega}_{,s} &= \Gamma_s \\ (D u_\alpha)_{,t} + (D u_\alpha u_\beta + \frac{1}{2} gD^2 \delta_{\alpha\beta} )_{,\beta} + (u_\alpha \tilde{\omega})_{,s} &= S_{q\alpha} + S_{h\alpha} + u_\alpha \Gamma_s \\ (Dw)_{,t} + (D u_\beta w)_{,\beta} + (w\tilde{\omega})_{,s} &= S_{qz} + w \Gamma_s\end{split}\]

The modified normal velocity \(\tilde{\omega}\) is given by

(1)¶\[ \tilde{\omega} = D ( s_{,t} + u_\alpha s_{,\alpha} + w s_{,z} )\]

and the inhomogeneous term \(\Gamma_s\) is given by

(2)¶\[\Gamma_s = D_{,t} + u_\alpha D_{,\alpha} + D ( (s_{,t})_{,s} + u_\alpha (s_{,\alpha})_{,s} + w (s_{,z})_{,s} )\]

There are three choices of \(s\) coordinate system in the model.

1) The original \(\sigma\) coordinate

In the original \(\sigma\) coordinate system, i.e., \(s = \sigma\),

\[\Gamma_s = 0\]

and

\[\tilde{\omega} = \omega = D (\sigma_{,t} + u_\alpha \sigma_{,\alpha} + w \sigma_{,z}).\]

2) \(s = s(\sigma)\)

The choice that \(s\) is a function of \(\sigma\) only is convenient in terms of minimizing the impact on the structure of the governing equations. In this case, the governing equations, retaining \(s\) derivatives and expressions in terms of \(s\) rather than \(\sigma\), are given by

\[\begin{split}D_{,t} + ( D u_\alpha )_{,\alpha} + s_{,\sigma}(\sigma_{,s}\tilde{\omega})_{,s} & = 0 \\ (D u_\alpha)_{,t} + (D u_\alpha u_\beta + \frac{1}{2} gD^2 \delta_{\alpha\beta} )_{,\beta} + s_{,\sigma}(\sigma_{,s} u_\alpha \tilde{\omega})_{,s} & = S_{q\alpha} + S_{h\alpha} \\ (Dw)_{,t} + (D u_\beta w)_{,\beta} + s_{,\sigma}(\sigma_{,s} w \tilde{\omega})_{,s} & = S_{qz}\end{split}\]

Use of the transformation and retention of \(s\) rather than \(\sigma\) in all expressions leads at most to the introduction of a variable coefficient outside the \(s\)-derivative terms in each equation.

3) General cases

For cases where \(s\) or \(\sigma\) can’t be expressed in terms of each other alone, the details of the transformation and the resulting expressions for \(\tilde{\omega}\) and \(\Gamma_s\) must be worked out for each individual case.
